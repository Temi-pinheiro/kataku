Usability passes

Five reworks, each tied to a line in the brief and the one specific user: one-handed, walking Bali streets in hard sun, interrupted every ten minutes, with an ear for what a real teacher sounds like.

Each opens as a before → after on iPhone frames, dark + light, on the existing token set. They also appear together under Usability in the Design System tab.

1
One-handed reach
Everything reachable by thumb
The most-tapped control — the mic — moves from stranded bottom-left into the thumb arc as a 60px primary. ✕ stays top-left, safe to miss. The whole taught card becomes the play target.

2
Daylight survival
Light theme must beat the sun
Under a simulated glare wash, faint grey narration vanishes. New contrast tokens (narration ink at ~9:1, deeper go-green) hold up; theme auto-follows ambient light.
Open →
3
Honest live mic
Never speak into a void · animated
The chat gets the orb's honesty: warming (ring breathes, no capture) → live (teal, level-driven bars, verbatim words) → done (editable, nothing auto-fired). The contradiction in the current design, fixed.
Open →
4
Leaving is safe
I end the utterance, not the OS
A half-spoken sentence survives a phone call and resumes in one tap, with no “welcome back.” In conversation, a labelled end-turn affordance replaces every countdown and auto-cutoff.
Open →
5
One focal element
The screen is a remote, not a document
History recedes to 40% with unlit play buttons; the current card lifts and owns the only glow. The spend figure goes inert — slate, no arrow, never pulsing.
